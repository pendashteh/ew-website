<h1 class="mb-2">Ambassador Program</h1>
<p class="lead text-secondary mb-5">You don't need to write code to help technology work for people. Ambassadors are the connected, values-driven people in our community who carry that message into the places technologists rarely reach.</p>

---

## What Is an Ambassador?

Ambassadors are not technologists — and that's the point. They are people who are embedded in communities, trusted in their circles, and genuinely curious about how technology can do good or cause harm. They want to understand enough to advocate well: to point people toward tools that help, and to name the risks when they see them.

## How the Program Works

The Ambassador Program is a three-way relationship between ambassadors, tech groups in the Electron Network, and Electron Workshop.

## For Tech Groups: How to Participate

If you're a tech group, individual, or organisation in the Electron Network with knowledge worth sharing, you can contribute to the Ambassador Program in ways that suit your capacity.

<div class="row g-3 my-3">

  <div class="col-12 col-md-6">
    <div class="d-flex gap-3 p-3 rounded border border-primary border-opacity-25 h-100">
      <i class="bi bi-camera-video-fill text-primary fs-4 flex-shrink-0 mt-1"></i>
      <div>
        <p class="fw-semibold mb-1">Run an Education Session</p>
        <p class="text-secondary mb-0 small">Lead a focused session for a small group of ambassadors. Sessions are practical and conversational — not lectures. You explain a topic, answer questions, and help people build enough understanding to represent it confidently.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="col-12 col-md-6">
    <div class="d-flex gap-3 p-3 rounded border border-primary border-opacity-25 h-100">
      <i class="bi bi-file-earmark-text-fill text-primary fs-4 flex-shrink-0 mt-1"></i>
      <div>
        <p class="fw-semibold mb-1">Contribute a Plain-Language Resource</p>
        <p class="text-secondary mb-0 small">Write or co-create a short explainer, FAQ, or guide on a topic you know well — written for a non-technical audience. These resources support ambassadors between sessions and give them something to share.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="col-12 col-md-6">
    <div class="d-flex gap-3 p-3 rounded border border-primary border-opacity-25 h-100">
      <i class="bi bi-chat-dots-fill text-primary fs-4 flex-shrink-0 mt-1"></i>
      <div>
        <p class="fw-semibold mb-1">Be a Point of Contact</p>
        <p class="text-secondary mb-0 small">Make yourself available for occasional questions from ambassadors who need a quick answer or a second opinion before speaking to their community. Low commitment, high value.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="col-12 col-md-6">
    <div class="d-flex gap-3 p-3 rounded border border-primary border-opacity-25 h-100">
      <i class="bi bi-people-fill text-primary fs-4 flex-shrink-0 mt-1"></i>
      <div>
        <p class="fw-semibold mb-1">Co-design the Curriculum</p>
        <p class="text-secondary mb-0 small">Help shape what topics ambassadors learn about. You know what's important in your domain — that expertise can guide the program toward what's genuinely useful to non-technical communities.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

</div>

Tech groups set their own level of involvement. A single session a year is a real contribution. There's no minimum.
